---
title: "Mutual Reserve: The Group's Welfare Fund"
description: A shared on-chain pot that cushions defaults — funded by small levies, paid out by objective rules, refunded at wind-down.
---

Every group is created with a **mutual reserve** — one `ReserveState` UTxO under
the treasury validator, identified by a permanent reserve token (`"RSVE"` + the
group's suffix). It is the on-chain form of the welfare fund most real-world
chamas already keep: everyone chips in a little, and the pot absorbs the shock
when a member drops out.

## Why it exists

When a defaulter is terminated, their forfeited collateral rarely covers what
they still owed the rotation — an early borrower can walk away owing most of a
lap. Without a reserve that loss is silently socialized: every later borrower
receives a pro-rata-shrunk pot. The reserve turns that implicit loss into an
explicit, pre-funded cushion with rules the validator enforces.

## Funding — two configurable levies

| Knob (`GroupDatum`) | When it fires | Real-world analogue |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `reserve_join_levy` | once, at each `joinGroup` | registration fee |
| `reserve_round_levy` | per contributing member, each `distributePayout` | per-meeting welfare contribution |

Both are denominated in the **contribution asset**, either may be `0`, and both
freeze once a member joins — like every other economic term. The round levy
comes out of the pot (the borrower receives `gross − levy_total`), so nobody
pays anything on top of their normal contribution.

Anyone may also donate at any time with `topUpReserve` (harambee) — the
validator only allows the pot to grow through that door. Note that a top-up
cannot share a transaction with a distribute round; sequence them.

## Paying out — objective rules only

The reserve has **no discretionary spending**. It moves money in exactly three
situations:

1. **Default cover (the stand-in).** `terminateDefault` routes the defaulter's
   forfeited balance INTO the reserve (defaulter's own assets first) and adds
   their remaining rounds this lap to a `standin_rounds` counter. While the
   counter is positive, each round draws `min(contribution_fee, pot)` into the
   payout — every remaining borrower gets the pot they were promised, up to
   what the reserve holds. The counter decrements every round **even when the
   pot is dry**, so owed cover always drains and a `beginRecommit` reset (which
   requires `standin_rounds == 0`) is always eventually reachable.
2. **Wind-down refunds.** Once the group is deactivated, each exiting member may
   take `floor(balance / pre-exit member_count)` on the way out
   (`exitGroup`'s `claimReserveShare`, on by default) — a self-adjusting equal
   split; remainders roll forward and the last exiter may take everything left.
   Exits while the group is **active** never touch the reserve: welfare
   contributions are insurance, not a deposit.
3. **Close-out.** `deleteGroup` burns the reserve token with the group tokens;
   any residue returns to the deleting admin as change.

## Honest limits

- The reserve can only pay out what was **pre-locked**. If it runs dry
  mid-cover, the draw degrades to whatever is left and the pro-rata rotation is
  the floor — on-chain code cannot force a pseudonymous defaulter to pay.
- Overlapping defaults **extend** the cover period rather than deepening it
  (one fee-unit per round, flat counter) — total cover is conserved.
- With the round levy off, the stand-in draw is carried by incentive: the
  borrower gains by including the reserve in the round, and the recommit gate
  waits for the counter regardless.

## SDK surface

```ts
// Configure at creation (both default 0n = off)
createGroup({ ..., groupDatum: { ...datum, reserve_join_levy: 500_000n, reserve_round_levy: 100_000n } })

// Donate — permissionless, increase-only
topUpReserve({ groupTokenSuffix, amount: 1_000_000n })

// Read the pot
const { balance, standinRounds, joinLevy, roundLevy } =
  await getReserveState(groupTokenSuffix)

// Wind-down exit: claim the equal share (default) or leave it
exitGroup({ groupTokenSuffix, claimReserveShare: true })
```

`joinGroup`, `distributePayout`, `terminateDefault`, and `deleteGroup` handle
their reserve legs automatically. Everything is anchored by the reserve token,
whose name is derivable off-chain from the group suffix alone
(`reserveTokenName(groupRefName)`).
